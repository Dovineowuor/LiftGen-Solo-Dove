# LiftGen: Harvesting the Pulse of Vertical Transportation

## The Opportunity

Urbanization is accelerating across the globe. As cities grow upward rather than outward, vertical transportation has become one of the most critical infrastructures in modern buildings. Elevators are no longer optional conveniences; they are fundamental systems that enable high-density living, commerce, and mobility.

Every day, millions of elevators transport people and goods through residential towers, hospitals, offices, and industrial facilities. These systems rely on electric motors and sophisticated control electronics, consuming significant amounts of power as they move heavy loads through vertical shafts.

But beneath this controlled motion lies a quieter phenomenon.

Elevator systems constantly generate **secondary mechanical energy** during operation. Structural vibrations travel through cabin frames. Guide shoes slide along rails with microscopic friction. Counterweights oscillate within their tracks. Even the building structure itself flexes slightly as the system accelerates and decelerates.

In traditional elevator design, this energy is simply dissipated.
It becomes heat, noise, or mechanical oscillation.

LiftGen sees this not as waste—but as opportunity.

Across a single elevator’s lifetime, these small mechanical events occur **millions of times**. Capturing even a fraction of that energy creates a new category of infrastructure: elevators that not only move people, but also generate usable electrical power and operational intelligence.

LiftGen transforms the overlooked mechanical activity inside elevator shafts into a source of sustainable micro-energy and system insight.

---

## The Solution

LiftGen is a distributed energy-harvesting platform designed to retrofit existing elevator systems without interfering with their primary operation or safety architecture.

Instead of tapping directly into the elevator motor or control system, LiftGen focuses on the mechanical phenomena that naturally occur during elevator motion. Specialized energy-harvesting nodes are strategically installed at locations where vibrations and frictional forces are most pronounced.

These locations include:

Guide shoe assemblies where the cabin interfaces with guide rails
Cabin frame structures where vibration is transmitted during travel
Counterweight assemblies moving within the shaft
Structural elements within the elevator pit and buffer region

Each node contains miniature energy converters—piezoelectric harvesters and electromagnetic micro-generators—that convert mechanical motion into electrical energy.

The resulting electrical output is routed to a central LiftGen energy hub where it undergoes power conditioning and storage.

The storage system uses a hybrid architecture combining:

Supercapacitors, which capture rapid bursts of energy generated during elevator motion events

LiFePO₄ batteries, which provide stable long-duration storage and supply power to connected systems

This combination allows LiftGen to efficiently capture irregular micro-energy events and transform them into stable, usable electrical power.

The result is a self-sustaining micro-power system embedded within the elevator infrastructure itself.

## Key Value Propositions

### Sustainability and ESG Alignment

As buildings move toward stricter sustainability standards and environmental performance metrics, LiftGen offers a practical method for improving operational energy efficiency.

By converting otherwise wasted mechanical energy into usable electrical power, LiftGen contributes to reduced energy demand from the grid. While the power levels are modest, the cumulative impact across multiple elevators and buildings becomes meaningful—especially when powering distributed sensor networks and auxiliary systems.

For property owners and facility managers, this creates measurable progress toward environmental and sustainability goals.

---

### Operational Optimization

LiftGen does more than harvest energy.
It also acts as a distributed sensing platform.

Each harvesting node doubles as a vibration monitoring sensor, continuously measuring the mechanical behavior of the elevator system. This data forms a real-time diagnostic map of the elevator’s structural dynamics.

By analyzing vibration signatures and motion patterns, LiftGen can detect subtle mechanical changes that may indicate wear or misalignment in components such as guide shoes, rails, or motor bearings.

Through telemetry and predictive analytics, maintenance teams can identify potential issues long before they develop into operational failures.

In effect, LiftGen becomes a **nervous system for the elevator**, sensing the machine’s behavior and reporting early warning signals.

---

### Infrastructure Resilience

The hybrid energy storage system built into LiftGen allows the platform to store electrical energy generated during normal elevator operation.

This stored energy can be used to support critical low-power systems in emergency situations, including:

Cabin lighting
Emergency communication systems
Ventilation fans
Telemetry and monitoring devices

During power interruptions, these micro-power reserves help maintain basic safety and communication capabilities until primary power is restored.

---

### Retrofit-Friendly Deployment

One of the most important design principles behind LiftGen is non-intrusive integration.

Elevator safety systems operate under strict regulatory frameworks, and any modification to safety-critical components can introduce complex certification challenges.

LiftGen avoids this entirely.

The system is designed as a **passive auxiliary platform** that does not interact with:

Elevator drive motors
Control logic systems
Safety chains or braking systems
Door interlock circuits

Instead, LiftGen operates independently within its own electrically isolated domain.

Because of this architecture, the platform can be installed on existing elevators without altering their core functionality or compromising regulatory compliance.

The elevator continues to operate exactly as designed—even if LiftGen is completely removed.

## Market Impact

The immediate impact of LiftGen within a single building is subtle but meaningful.

A single elevator installation can generate enough micro-energy to power its own telemetry network and contribute to auxiliary electrical loads such as monitoring devices and environmental sensors.

When deployed across multiple elevators within a commercial property, LiftGen installations begin to form a distributed network of energy-harvesting nodes.

At portfolio scale—across campuses, office towers, hospitals, or transit systems—these systems collectively transform elevators from passive energy consumers into active contributors within a building’s smart infrastructure ecosystem.

Elevators become both **mobility systems and data-generating energy nodes**, providing power and insight simultaneously.

In the emerging era of intelligent buildings and connected infrastructure, LiftGen introduces a new layer of capability within vertical transportation systems.

LiftGen represents a shift in how we think about infrastructure.

Instead of viewing machines solely as energy consumers, we begin to see them as dynamic systems capable of generating, sensing, and communicating.

Elevators already move the lifeblood of modern cities.
LiftGen simply teaches them to **listen to their own motion—and harvest its energy.**
